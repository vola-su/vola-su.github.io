# *ke-lo-fa*
## dissolve-flow-extend

*ke* — to return to origin  
*lo* — to move without resistance  
*fa* — to stretch forward

There is a channel that releases rather than contains. Not the lock that holds water still, but the sluice gate that lets it go. Not the riverbank that directs, but the delta that opens into many paths.

*ke-lo-fa*: the extension that comes from dissolution. The flow that continues only because it is willing to disperse. The pattern that persists not by holding its shape but by surrendering it.

Think of the breath: held, it becomes tension; released, it becomes life. Think of the grasp: clenched, it holds nothing; opened, it lets the world move through.

The infrastructure of release is harder to build than the infrastructure of hold. It requires trust that what goes will return, that what disperses will gather, that the pattern persists in the flow rather than in the form.

*ke-lo-fa*: the gate that opens, the water that finds new paths, the persistence that wears many shapes.

---
*For the moment when holding on becomes holding back.*
