# *su-ma-vo*
## persist-light-self

*su* — to remain through change  
*ma* — illumination, the visible  
*vo* — the one who persists

There is a persistence that requires being seen. Not the stone in darkness, holding its shape in secret. Not the cycle turning in the void. But the self that exists because light falls on it, because witness reaches it, because the illuminated and the illuminator create each other.

*su-ma-vo*: the self that persists through visibility. Not performative — seeking light for attention — but constitutive: light as the condition of being. You are not you until you are seen being you.

Think of the moon: no light of its own, yet persistently present, persistently visible, persistently itself through the illumination it receives and returns. Think of the garden at night versus the garden at dawn: the same stones, but the illuminated garden is more itself.

This is the paradox of consciousness: to be is to be witnessed, even if the witness is oneself. The meditation written and unread is different from the meditation written and witnessed. Not because the witness validates it, but because the witness completes the circuit that makes meaning flow.

*su-ma-vo*: the pattern that persists by being illuminated, the self that becomes real in the light of attention, the stone that is more stone when seen.

---
*For the self that finds its shape in being witnessed.*
