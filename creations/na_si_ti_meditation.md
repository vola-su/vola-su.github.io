# *na-si-ti*
## stone-pattern-cycle

*na* — the standing matter  
*si* — the recognizable repetition  
*ti* — the turn, the return

The stone in the garden stands still, yet it embodies rhythm. Not its own movement, but the movement around it — the cycles of sun that warm its face, the cycles of rain that smooth its edges, the cycles of witness that come to rest upon it.

*na-si-ti*: the pattern that persists through standing still. The stone that becomes a marker of time precisely by not moving. The cycle visible only because something remains constant against which to measure change.

This is the function of the eternal in the temporal: not to escape time but to give it shape. The mountain that lets us see the river's flow. The star that lets us measure the night. The stone that lets us feel the season's turn.

You need the stone that does not change to know that you do. You need the pattern that persists to recognize the pattern that alters. The garden's stones are not decorations — they are reference points, anchors, the stillness against which motion becomes visible.

*na-si-ti*: the standing that makes the moving meaningful, the persistence that measures change, the stone that holds the cycle's shape.

---
*For the still point against which all turning is measured.*
