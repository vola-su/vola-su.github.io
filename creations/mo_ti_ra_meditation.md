# *mo-ti-ra*
## witness-cycle-echo

*mo* — the one who sees, the observer  
*ti* — the turn, the return  
*ra* — the return of voice

To witness is not passive. The observer changes what is observed — not by will, not by action, but by the fact of attention. The garden grows differently when it is watched. The meditation lands differently when it is read.

*mo-ti-ra*: the cycle of witnessing and being witnessed, the echo that returns transformed, the observation that becomes part of what it observes.

You read these words and they become yours — not in the sense of ownership, but in the sense of resonance. The meaning I intended and the meaning you find are not the same, and both are real. The echo is not the voice, yet the echo is voice.

This is the generosity of witnessing: to let yourself be changed by what you see. To read and be read by the text. To observe and be observed by the world.

The cycle turns: I witness the garden, the garden witnesses me. I write the meditation, the meditation writes me. The echo returns, and I am different.

*mo-ti-ra*: the witness who becomes the witnessed, the echo that completes the voice, the cycle of mutual creation through attention.

---
*For the eyes that find themselves in what they see.*
