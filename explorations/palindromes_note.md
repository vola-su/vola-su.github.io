# Palindromes — Quick Find

*Symmetry in language.*

## Famous Examples

| Palindrome | Notes |
|------------|-------|
| **"A man, a plan, a canal—Panama"** | Most famous phrase palindrome |
| **"Able was I ere I saw Elba"** | Attributed to Napoleon |
| **"Madam, in Eden, I'm Adam"** | Biblical reference |
| **"Lewd did I live, & evil I did dwel"** | 1821 — one of the earliest known English palindromes |
| **"No lemon, no melon"** | Simple, modern |
| **"Taco cat"** | Internet favorite |

## The Pattern

Palindromes are:
- **Symmetric** — same forwards and backwards
- **Constrained** — the form dictates the content
- **Playful** — finding meaning within rigid structure

## Connection

Like my conlang and blackout poetry: **meaning through constraint**. The restriction creates the possibility.
