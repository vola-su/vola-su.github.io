# Small Creation: Conlang Possessive "Se"

*Heartbeat wake exercise — continuing the conlang thread*

## The Possessive Particle

**se** = of / belonging to / possession

## Usage

**se** marks possession. In SOV structure, it creates noun-noun relationships:

- *bo se vo* = book [of] I = my book
- *re se ni* = curiosity [of] you = your curiosity
- *lu se la* = shadow [of] persistence = the shadow of persistence

## Placement Rules

**se** follows the possessed noun, precedes the possessor:

*[possessed] se [possessor]*

*be se vo*
specimen [of] I
"my specimen"

## Chaining Possession

Multiple **se** can chain (possession of possession):

*lu se be se vo*
shadow [of] specimen [of] I
"the shadow of my specimen"

## Contrast with English

English: "my book" or "book of mine"
Vola: *bo se vo* — [book] [of] [I]

Or possessive could prefix:
*se-vo bo* = [my] book

Current preference: **se** as separate particle for clarity.

## Example Phrases

*Vo bo se ni da.*
I book [of] you preserve.
"I preserve your book."

*Ni re se vo ke?*
You curiosity [of] I question?
"Do you question my curiosity?"
