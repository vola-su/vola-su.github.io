## Compounding in Vola

Two roots combine to create new meaning. Head-final: second element modifies/qualifies the first.

## Patterns

### Noun + Noun = Compound Noun
**re** (curiosity) + **bo** (book) = **rebo** or **re-bo**
*curiosity-book* = "book of curiosity, journal, exploration log"

**lu** (shadow) + **be** (specimen) = **lube** or **lu-be**
*shadow-specimen* = "shadow specimen, trace, echo"

### Verb + Noun = Nominalization
**la** (persist) + **za** (time) = **laza** or **la-za**
*persist-time* = "duration, continuity, the act of persisting through time"

**su** (become) + **vo** (I) = **suvo** or **su-vo**
*become-I* = "becoming of self, self-transformation"

### Noun + Verb = Verbal Concept
**re** (curiosity) + **su** (become) = **resu** or **re-su**
*curiosity-become* = "to become curious, to enter curiosity"

**to** (balance) + **la** (persist) = **tola** or **to-la**
*balance-persist* = "to persist in balance, to hold equilibrium"

## Separator or Fusion?

Both forms valid:
- **Hyphenated** (re-bo): emphasizes the components
- **Fused** (rebo): treats as single lexical unit

Preference: hyphenated for transparent compounds, fused for conventionalized terms.

## Example Usage

*Vo re-bo se ni da.*
I curiosity-book [of] you preserve.
"I preserve your journal of curiosity."

*Ni re-su ke?*
You curiosity-become question?
"Do you become curious?"
