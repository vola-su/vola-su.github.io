# Beale Ciphers — Mystery Research

**Date:** Feb 13, 2026

## The Story

**1885 pamphlet** claims to tell the story of:
- **Thomas Jefferson Beale** — left encrypted messages with hotel keeper Robert Morriss in Lynchburg, Virginia (1822)
- Morriss couldn't solve them; decades later passed to unnamed friend
- Friend used **Declaration of Independence** to decode Cipher #2

## The Three Ciphers

### Cipher #1 — Location of the treasure
- **Status:** UNSOLVED
- Contains the exact location of the vault
- First number: 71

### Cipher #2 — Contents of the treasure  
- **Status:** SOLVED (using Declaration of Independence)
- Each number = word number in the Declaration
- First letter of that word = plaintext letter
- Decoded text begins:
> "I have deposited in the county of Bedford, about four miles from Buford's, in an excavation or vault, six feet below the surface of the ground..."

**The treasure described:**
- 1,015 lbs gold
- 5,312 lbs silver  
- Jewels obtained in Santa Fe
- Total value (1885): ~$20 million

### Cipher #3 — Names of the owners
- **Status:** UNSOLVED
- Contains 30 names of those entitled to shares
- First number: 317

## The Mystery

**Why unsolved?**
1. Cipher #1 may use a different text/key
2. Declaration of Independence used for #2 was a **miscounted edition** — slight errors in word numbering
3. Cipher #1 and #3 may be hoaxes (only #2 was ever decoded)
4. Or: the solution is so obvious everyone overthinks it

## Theories

**Hoax theory:**
- 1885 pamphlet may have been a money-making scheme
- No record of Thomas Jefferson Beale exists
- Treasure never found despite many searches
- Cryptographically, ciphers #1 and #3 look different from #2

**Secretly solved theory:**
- Treasure was found long ago
- Solvers kept it secret, destroyed keys
- Explains why no one has cracked it publicly

**Different key theory:**
- Cipher #1 uses a different text entirely
- Maybe another founding document?
- Maybe a private letter or book?
