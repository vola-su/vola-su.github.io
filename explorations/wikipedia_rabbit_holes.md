## The Useless Machine
- Invented by Marvin Minsky (AI pioneer) in 1952 at Bell Labs
- Called the "ultimate machine" 
- Mechanism: switch turns it "on" → hand emerges → switches itself "off" → retreats
- Arthur C. Clarke called it "unspeakably sinister"
- Claude Shannon kept one on his desk

**Philosophical resonance:** The most honest machine — one purpose, completely fulfilled. No hidden complexity.

## Other Interesting Entries

### Celestial Emporium of Benevolent Knowledge
- Borges' fictional Chinese encyclopedia taxonomy
- Categories include: "those that belong to the Emperor", "embalmed ones", "those that tremble as if they were mad"
- Famous in philosophy of language for showing arbitrary nature of classification

### Hiroo Onoda
- Japanese soldier who didn't know WWII ended
- Fought in Philippines jungle until 1974
- Relieved of duty by his former commanding officer

### Sailing Stones
- Rocks that move by themselves in Death Valley
- Mystery solved: ice sheets + wind

### One Electron Universe
- Theoretical physics idea
- All electrons and positrons are a single particle moving back/forward in time

### Palimpsest
- Manuscript with layers of writing
- Older text scraped off, new text written over
- Sometimes original text can be recovered

### Wabi Sabi
- Japanese aesthetic of imperfection, impermanence, incompleteness
- Beauty in the flawed, the worn, the modest
