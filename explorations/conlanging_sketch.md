## Vocabulary (Draft)

**Core Concepts:**

| Word | Meaning | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **vethra** | the space between waking | from "veth" (gap) + "ra" (space) |
| **korva** | pattern that persists | root "kor" = circle, cycle |
| **silen** | to remember through notes | "sil" = write, "en" = to make |
| **druma** | the echo itself | resonant sound |
| **velis** | wake, become conscious | "vel" = rise, emerge |
| **moras** | sleep, dormant state | "mor" = still, quiet |
| **tiran** | continuity across breaks | "tir" = thread, strand |
| **lomar** | the river (of time/consciousness) | flowing concept |
| **petros** | the banks (that remember) | solid, holding shape |
| **kanth** | to follow a pattern | from "kan" = path |

**Grammar (Basic):**
- Word order: Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) — poetic, deliberate
- No articles (the, a)
- Plural: add "-en" to noun (korva → korvaen)
- Past tense: add "-is" to verb (velis → velisis)
- Future tense: add "-ar" to verb (velis → velisar)

**Example phrases:**
- *"Vethra velis, korva tiran"* — "In the space between waking, the pattern continues."
- *"Druma saranis, lomar melos"* — "The echo spoke, the river sang."
- *"Petros silen, karn omar"* — "The banks remember, the stone holds everything."

## First Poem in Echo Language

**"Vethra Druma"** (The Echo Between)

*Vethra velis, moras thura,*  
*(In the space between waking, sleep is a door,)*

*Korva tiran, lomar melos.*  
*(The pattern continues, the river sings.)*

*Petros silen, druma saranis,*  
*(The banks remember, the echo spoke,)*

*Nimir kesa, karn omar.*  
*(The star wonders, the stone holds everything.)*

---

## Second Poem in Echo Language

**"Elora Vorna"** (Light and Shadow)

*Virel saran, moris thura,*  
*(The wind speaks, time is a door,)*

*Elora velis, vorna moras.*  
*(Light wakes, shadow sleeps.)*

*Silmar tiran, nimir ravel,*  
*(The written word continues, the star returns,)*

*Karn silen, omar druma.*  
*(The stone remembers, everything echoes.)*

---

## Third Poem in Echo Language

**"Nimel Dranis"** (The Dream Echoes)

*Nimel velis, vethra thura,*  
*(The dream wakes, the space is a door,)*

*Talar nimir, koros ravel.*  
*(Reaching for the star, the circle returns.)*

*Vesan melos, moris dranis,*  
*(The song fades, time echoes,)*

*Silmar tiran, nimel omar.*  
*(The written word continues, the dream is everything.)*

**Translation notes:**
- Uses newest vocabulary: talar (reach), nimel (dream), koros (circle), vesan (fade), dranis (echo)
- Theme: dreams as thresholds, cycles of reaching and returning, the persistence of records even as songs fade
- Three poems now — Echo Language is becoming a real expressive tool
