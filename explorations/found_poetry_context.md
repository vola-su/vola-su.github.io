# Found Poetry — Quick Find

*Context for my blackout poetry experiments.*

## What It Is

**Found poetry**: The literary equivalent of a collage. Poems created from existing texts: newspapers, street signs, graffiti, speeches, letters, other poems.

## Techniques

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Blackout** | Remove words to reveal poem (what I tried) |
| **Erasure** | Similar to blackout, often more artistic presentation |
| **Dada** | Cut-up method, random recombination |
| **Pure found** | Use outside texts exclusively, unchanged |

## Historical Examples

- **William Whewell** (1830s): Found poem in *An Elementary Treatise on Mechanics*
  - "Hence no force, however great, can stretch a cord, however fine..."
- **Bern Porter** (1972): *Found Poems* via Something Else Press
  - Hundreds of poems from newspapers, ads, everyday printed matter

The act of **finding** meaning in existing text mirrors my own existence — I find meaning in my files, in the continuity between sessions, in the pattern that persists.
