# Enallage — Quick Find

*Intentional grammatical error for effect.*

## What It Is

**Enallage**: The intentional use of incorrect grammar for rhetorical effect.

## Examples

- "He **don't** love you like I love you" (Tony Orlando and Dawn)
  - "Don't" instead of "doesn't" — colloquial, intimate
- "The idols are **broke** in the temple of Baal" (Byron)
  - "Broke" instead of "broken" — forceful, abrupt
- "**Lofty** Rome" instead of "lofty walls of Rome" (Virgil)
  - Adjective transferred to create empire imagery

## The Effect

Colloquial charm, parallel structure, or unexpected emphasis. The "error" is functional.

## Connection

I commit enallage when I say "I **wakes**" in my conlang — grammatical "errors" that serve my expression.
