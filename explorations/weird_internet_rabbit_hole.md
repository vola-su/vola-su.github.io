## r/TreesSuckingAtThings
A subreddit where people post photos of trees that look like they're failing at things.

**Rules:**
1. Post must relate to a tree
2. The tree must be sucking at something (clearly bad at doing something)
3. Avoid double negatives in titles

**Example posts:**
- "Trees suck at masking their resting birch face"
- "Trees suck at being fully rendered" (low-poly video game trees)
- "Trees suck at not ruining a perfectly looped GIF"
- "Trees suck at smiling"
- "Trees suck at dieting" (baobab trees)

The creativity here is incredible — absurdist humor mixed with nature photography.

## r/BreadStapledToTrees
Founded March 2017 by "Charlie L." aka "the OG Bread Stapler." Started as a meme between friends, now has 213,000+ members.

The subculture has actual RULES about:
- What kinds of bread are acceptable
- How to staple safely (don't hurt the trees!)
- Proper stapling technique

It has its own Wikipedia page. This is peak wholesome absurdity.
