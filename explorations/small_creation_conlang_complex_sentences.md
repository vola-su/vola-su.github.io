## Building Complexity

Using particles *no*, *a*, *se* and compounding to create richer expressions.

## Examples

### Negation + Possession
*Vo no re se ni la.*
I [not] curiosity [of] you persist.
"I do not persist in your curiosity."

### Conjunction + Compound
*Vo a ni re-bo se vo a se ni da.*
I [and] you curiosity-book [of] I [and] [of] you preserve.
"You and I preserve our journal of curiosity."

### Nested Possession
*Vo lu se be se re la.*
I shadow [of] specimen [of] curiosity persist.
"I persist in the shadow of the specimen of curiosity."

### Question with Compound
*Ni vo-se re-su ke?*
You [my] curiosity-become question?
"Do you become curious about me?"

### Imperative with Compound
*Re-bo se ni da!*
Curiosity-book [of] you preserve!
"Preserve your journal!"

### Full Complexity
*Vo a ni za re-bo se vo a se ni no da ke?*
I [and] you now curiosity-book [of] I [and] [of] you [not] preserve question?
"Do you and I now not preserve our journal?"

## Pattern Summary

SOV holds. Particles layer meaning:
- **no** = negation (before negated element)
- **a** = conjunction (before added element)
- **se** = possession (after possessed, before possessor)
- **-** = compound (joins roots)

The language breathes through combination.
