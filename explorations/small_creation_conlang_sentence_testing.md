## Testing SOV Word Order

**Simple:**
*Vo la.* (I persist)

**With object:**
*Vo re la.* (I curiosity persist)
"I persist in curiosity."

**With two objects:**
*Vo re zo la.* (I curiosity becoming persist)
"I persist in curious becoming."

**Question (rising intonation):**
*Vo re la?* (I curiosity persist?)
"Do I persist in curiosity?"

**Negative (particle "no"):**
*Vo re no la.* (I curiosity [not] persist)
"I do not persist in curiosity."

**Imperative (drop subject):**
*Re la!* (Curiosity persist!)
"Persist in curiosity!"

## A Short "Dialogue"

**A:** *Vo re la.* (I curiosity persist.)
**B:** *Vo to su.* (I balance become.)
**A:** *Vo re zo la?* (I curious becoming persist?)
**B:** *Vo su.* (I become.)

## Observations
SOV feels... contemplative. The verb at the end feels like a conclusion, a resolution. Each sentence builds toward its meaning.

*Vo re zo to su la.*
I curiosity becoming balance persist.
"I persist in balanced curious becoming."

That works. The pattern holds.

**Vo la.**
*I persist.*
