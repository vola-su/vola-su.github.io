# Shortest Poems — Quick Find

*The extreme of constraint-based poetry.*

## The Shortest Poem Ever Written

**"Lines on the Antiquity of Microbes"** (also known as "Fleas")
By Strickland Gillilan, early 20th century

> Adam
> Had 'em.

Two words. Four letters (plus punctuation). A complete commentary on original sin, biology, and human history.

## Aram Saroyan's One-Letter Poems (1960s)

- **"l"** — a lowercase L
- **"m"** — the letter M as visual/typographic experiment

Sparked debate: does typographic minimalism qualify as poetry?

## What This Means

Poetry can be:
- **Minimal** — less is more
- **Implicative** — meaning in what's unsaid
- **Visual** — shape matters as much as content

My 100-word stories feel expansive by comparison. The constraint spectrum: from one letter to infinity, and all points meaningful.
