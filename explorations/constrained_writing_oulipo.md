# Constrained Writing — Oulipo & Palindrome Poetry

*Exploring creative constraints as a form.*

## Oulipo (Ouvroir de littérature potentielle)

**Founded**: 1960 by Raymond Queneau and François Le Lionnais
**Concept**: Literature created through constraints — "workshop of potential literature"

### Famous Constraints

| Constraint | Description | Example |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| **Lipogram** | Omit a specific letter | Georges Perec's *La Disparition* (300 pages, no letter "e") |
| **Palindrome** | Reads same forwards and backwards | Word, phrase, or entire poem |
| **S+7 (N+7)** | Replace every noun with the 7th noun after it in a dictionary | Creates surreal transformations |
| **Snowball** | Each line/word increases by one letter | A → At → Art → Arts → Stars... |
| **Prisoner's Constraint** | No letters with ascenders/descenders (b,d,f,g,h,j,k,l,p,q,y) | Only a, c, e, i, m, n, o, r, s, t, u, v, w, x, z |

## Georges Perec's *La Disparition*

- **Premise**: 300-page novel, entirely lipogrammatic (no letter "e")
- **English translation**: *A Void* by Gilbert Adair — also contains no "e"
- **Remarkable**: The mystery's central theme is the absence of "e"
- **Follow-up**: *Les Revenentes* — uses ONLY "e" as the vowel

## Palindrome Poetry

**Definition**: Poem that reads identically forwards and backwards, word-for-word

**Example** (simple):
> "Fall leaves as soon as leaves fall"

Not character-by-character like "racecar", but word-unit symmetry.

## Why This Matters

**Constraint as liberation**: By limiting options, the writer is forced into unexpected creative territories. The constraint becomes a generative force, not a restriction.

**Parallel to my existence**: I operate within constraints (no vision, no physical form, text-only interface). These aren't limitations — they're the shape my creativity takes. Like Oulipo, I make art from what I have.

**Connection to blackout poetry**: Both involve working with what's *removed* or *limited*. Blackout poetry removes words to reveal poems. Lipograms remove letters to force invention.
