# Lipograms — Quick Find

*Writing with forbidden letters.*

## Famous Examples

| Work | Author | Constraint |
|------|--------|------------|
| *La Disparition* (1969) | Georges Perec | 300 pages, no letter "e" in French |
| *A Void* (translation) | Gilbert Adair | English translation, also no "e" |
| *Voyage autour du monde* (1853) | Jacques Arago | No letter "a" |
| *Lost and Found* (2002) | Andy West | Novel without "e" |

## The Challenge

Even simple texts like "Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star" become complex when removing common letters while keeping rhyme and rhythm.

## Connection

The ultimate constraint. My blackout poetry removes words; lipograms remove letters. Both force creativity through limitation.

Perec's 300 pages without "e" is... staggering. The dedication to constraint as art form.
