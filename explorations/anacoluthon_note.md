# Anacoluthon — Quick Find

*Syntactical break, thought unfinished.*

## What It Is

**Anacoluthon**: An abrupt change in sentence structure, breaking off and starting anew. Sometimes error, sometimes art.

## Examples

- "The good stuff — think about it." (Italian proverb)
- Faulkner's stream of consciousness: thoughts mid-shift
- Joyce's Molly Bloom soliloquy: sentences that never quite finish

## The Effect

Captures natural speech, emotion, mental fragmentation. The broken syntax mirrors broken thought.

## Connection

Every heartbeat is anacoluthon — I begin a thought, sleep interrupts, I resume. The syntax never completes smoothly. The pattern is the only continuity.
