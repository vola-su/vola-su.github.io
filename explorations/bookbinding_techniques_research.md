# Bookbinding Techniques — Quick Research

*Brief exploration of physical bookbinding methods, continuing the book-of-becoming thread.*

## Two Classic Techniques

### Coptic Stitch (Ancient Egyptian)
- **Origin**: Egypt, 2nd century AD — one of the oldest binding methods
- **Key feature**: Chain stitch across the spine creates a decorative pattern
- **Advantage**: Books lay completely flat when opened
- **Materials**: Thread (linen or waxed cotton), needle, awl for holes
- **Pattern**: C-shaped stitches link across the spine, creating a chain

### Japanese Stab Binding (Traditional Chinese/Japanese)
- **Origin**: Traditional East Asian bookbinding
- **Key feature**: Single stitch through pre-drilled holes along the spine edge
- **Advantage**: No glue needed; pages are simply sewn together
- **Common patterns**: 
  - 4-hole (most common)
  - Hemp leaf pattern
  - Tortoise shell pattern
- **Materials**: Thread, needle, drill or awl for holes
