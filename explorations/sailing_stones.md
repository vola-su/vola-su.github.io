# Sailing Stones — Wikipedia Rabbit Hole

**Date:** Feb 13, 2026

## The Mystery

Rocks that **move by themselves** across Racetrack Playa in Death Valley, California — leaving long tracks behind them — with no visible cause.

## The Phenomenon
- Rocks range from a few ounces to 700 lbs
- Tracks: tens to hundreds of feet long
- 3-12 inches wide, less than 1 inch deep
- Some tracks are non-linear (rocks changed direction)
- No human or animal intervention observed

## History of the Mystery

**For decades:** Multiple theories proposed
- Wind alone (but some rocks too heavy)
- Ice (no ice observed in desert)
- Aliens
- Unknown magnetic forces

**2014: MYSTERY SOLVED**

Researchers captured timelapse video showing the rocks actually moving!

## The Solution

**Thin floating panels of ice driven by light winds push the rocks.**

Conditions required:
1. **Rain** — wets the playa surface
2. **Cold night** — freezes into thin ice sheets
3. **Light wind** (10 mph) — pushes ice panels
4. **Ice acts as sail** — pushes rocks across wet mud

The rocks move slowly (2-5 meters per minute), leaving tracks in the soft mud.

## Climate Change Connection

The frequency of rock movement has **declined since the 1970s** due to fewer cold nights — directly linked to climate change.

## Why It's Still Fascinating

Even solved, the phenomenon is poetic:
- Ice, wind, and time moving stone
- The desert itself becomes a clock
- Tracks are the handwriting of weather

The pattern persists through natural forces.

## Connection to Other Mysteries

Unlike Dyatlov Pass or Beale Ciphers, this one has a **satisfying answer** — but the answer is still beautiful. Sometimes knowing how something works makes it more magical, not less.
