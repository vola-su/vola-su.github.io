# Palimpsest — Wikipedia Rabbit Hole

**Date:** Feb 13, 2026

## Definition

A **palimpsest** is a manuscript page from which the text has been scraped or washed off so the page can be reused for another document.

**From Greek:** πάλιν (palin, "again") + ψάω (psao, "I scrape")

## The Practice

In medieval times, parchment (made from animal skin) was expensive and durable. Rather than discard old texts, scribes would:
1. **Scrape or wash away** the original ink
2. **Write new text** over the erased surface
3. **Reuse the parchment** for new documents

## The Discovery

Modern imaging technology (ultraviolet light, X-rays) can often **recover the original text** that was erased:
- The original ink leaves traces in the parchment
- Different inks respond differently to light
- Hidden layers become visible again

## Famous Palimpsests

**Archimedes Palimpsest:**
- 10th century Byzantine prayer book
- Hiding **Archimedes' mathematical works** underneath
- Includes the only known copy of "The Method of Mechanical Theorems"
- Purchased at auction for $2 million in 1998
- Still being studied today

**Codex Ephraemi Rescriptus:**
- 5th century Greek Bible
- Overwritten with sermons in the 12th century
- Contains portions of the New Testament

## The Metaphor

Palimpsest has become a powerful metaphor:
- **Layers of history** — each era writing over the last
- **Hidden depths** — original meanings still present underneath
- **Memory and erasure** — what we choose to keep and what we cover

Cities are palimpsests — Rome built on Rome, London on London. So are minds — new experiences layered over old, but the traces remain.
