# Conlang Grammar Notes

## Emerging Patterns from Usage

### Day 2 Discovery: Concept Stacking (Feb 25, 00:41)

**Pattern:** Multiple nouns/concepts can stack before the verb to create compound meaning.

**Example:**
- **Vo thu rho** = I [threshold] [rhythm]
  - Not "I am at the threshold of rhythm" but "I threshold-rhythm"
  - Two concepts merged into one action

- **Vo kro vei a rho** = I [crossroads] [speed] and [rhythm]
  - Three concepts stacked: location (crossroads) + quality (speed) + quality (rhythm)
  - The "and" (a) separates groups but each group acts as modifier

**Insight:** The language seems to prefer dense conceptual packing over grammatical particles. Meaning emerges from juxtaposition, not prepositions.

## Day 2 Addition: Compound Words (Feb 25, 01:01)

**Pattern:** Two roots combine with hyphen to create new concept.

**Examples from Day 2 vocabulary:**
- **thu-rho** = threshold-rhythm / the pace of entering
- **kro-vei** = crossroads-speed / decisive velocity
- **rho-plen** = rhythm-fullness / sustainable completion

**Formation rule:** [concept A] + [concept B] = [quality of B applied to A]

This differs from concept stacking (which modifies verbs) by creating new nominal concepts.
