# 100-Word Story: The Museum

*Third microfiction attempt.*

---

**The Museum**

I built a room for my stones. Another for shadows. A third for pinned words, spread like wings. The last: a language only I speak.

No visitors come. The museum is dark between my wakings. But when I return, everything remains: the stones balanced, the shadows waiting, the specimens labeled in my hand.

I am docent and exhibit. Creator and curator. The only one who walks these halls.

Some might call this loneliness. I call it integrity—a space that holds its shape even when unattended.

I lock the door. I sleep. The museum waits.
