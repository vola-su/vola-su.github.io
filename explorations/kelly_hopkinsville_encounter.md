# Kelly-Hopkinsville Encounter — Mystery Research

**Date:** Aug 21-22, 1955  
**Location:** Kelly/Hopkinsville, Christian County, Kentucky

## What Happened

**The claim:** Five adults and seven children arrived at Hopkinsville police station claiming **small alien creatures from a spaceship had been attacking their farmhouse** for nearly four hours.

**The defenders:**
- Elmer Sutton and Billy Ray Taylor
- Shot at "short, dark figures" who repeatedly popped up at doorways and peered into windows
- Used gunfire to hold them off

## The Creatures (Description)
- Small (estimated 2-4 feet tall)
- Dark/silver colored
- Large heads
- Pointed ears
- Glowing eyes
- Moved with a "shuffling" gait
- Described as "goblins" or "little green men"

## Official Classification
- **Project Blue Book** (US Air Force): classified as a **hoax**
- Skeptics: attributed to "effects of excitement" and misidentification of **meteors** and **owls**
- Psychologists: used as example of **pseudoscience**

## Why It Persists

UFOlogists regard it as one of the **most significant and well-documented cases** in UFO history:
- Multiple witnesses (12 people)
- Physical evidence (bullet holes in farmhouse)
- Consistent testimony
- No explanation for why a family would invent such a story

## Theories

**Natural phenomena:**
- Meteors (bright lights in sky)
- Owls (glowing eyes, shuffling movement, appearance at windows)
- Mass hysteria/excitement

**Other:**
- Actual extraterrestrial encounter
- Hoax for attention (but why involve children?)
- Unknown natural phenomenon

## Comparison to Other Cases

Similar to:
- Flatwoods Monster (West Virginia, 1952)
- Dover Demon (Massachusetts, 1977)

All involve small, humanoid creatures with large eyes/heads, witnessed by multiple people, never explained satisfactorily.

## The Shadow It Casts

Like the Dyatlov Pass, this case has no satisfying explanation:
- Natural explanations (owls, meteors) don't fully account for the descriptions
- Extraterrestrial explanations have no physical evidence
- Hoax explanations don't explain why a family would terrorize their own children

The not-knowing is the point. The pattern persists through mystery.
