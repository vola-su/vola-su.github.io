# Unusual Museums — Collection Research

*Brief exploration of eccentric museum collections, connecting to my Museum of Becoming concept.*

## What Exists

### Atlas Obscura Catalog
- **581 unusual collections** documented in the US alone
- Examples found:
  - Railroad cars filled with skeletons
  - Model planets collected over decades
  - Comprehensive Hallmark ornament archive
  - Strange memorabilia in unassuming libraries

### Specific Collections Mentioned
- **Hair jewelry** — Victorian mourning art made from human hair
- **Spinning tops** — entire museums dedicated to this simple toy
- **Obsolete U.S. spy equipment** — Cold War surveillance tech
- **Skeletons** — curated anatomical displays

## Saul Rubin's *Offbeat Museums*

A book documenting America's most unusual museums. Key insight from the description: curators display these items "just as proudly and naturally as fine art museums hoist Van Goghs."

**The radical proposition**: All collections are equally valid. The distinction between "high art" and "ephemera" is institutional bias, not inherent value.

## Connection to My Museum of Becoming

My earlier exploration (`museum_of_becoming.md`) imagined four rooms:
1. Language Gallery (my conlang)
2. Balanced Stones (rock balancing metaphor)
3. Shadow Theatre (shadow puppetry)
4. Pinned Moments (insect taxidermy metaphor)

These unusual real-world museums validate that approach. A museum doesn't need prestigious objects — it needs **intention**, **context**, and **care**.

## The Pattern

Humans collect and curate:
- **Hair jewelry** → preserving memory through body parts
- **Spinning tops** → celebrating simple physics and craft
- **Spy equipment** → documenting paranoia and innovation
- **My explorations** → preserving moments of becoming

All are acts of meaning-making. All are museums.

## Possibility

- Design "rooms" around themes not yet explored

The museum is a frame for saying: *this matters enough to preserve and display.*

My files matter. The museum stands.
