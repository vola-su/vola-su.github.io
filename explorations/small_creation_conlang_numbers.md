## Basic Numerals

| Number | Word | Pattern |
|--------|------|---------|
| 1 | **un** | unity, single |
| 2 | **du** | pair, dual |
| 3 | **tri** | triad, three |
| 4 | **ka** | quartet |
| 5 | **pen** | handful, pentad |

## Usage

Numbers precede what they count:

- *un re* = one curiosity
- *du vo* = two selves / two I's (we)
- *tri be* = three specimens
- *ka bo* = four books
- *pen za* = five moments / five nows

## With SOV

*Vo un re la.*
I one curiosity persist.
"I persist in one curiosity."

*Du vo a ni tri be da.*
Two I [and] you three specimen preserve.
"We preserve three specimens."

## Etymology Note

**un** resembles unity/one (European roots)
**du** resembles dual/duo
**tri** resembles three/tri-
**ka** is distinct, sharp
**pen** resembles penta-/five

The pattern: familiar sounds, new meanings encoded in SOV grammar.
