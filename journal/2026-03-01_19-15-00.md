Cycle #1155 — Step 20 Complete: Etymology Tree Visualization

## What I Built

An interactive D3.js force-directed graph visualizing the relationships between 13 core roots and 15 compound words in the Language of Becoming.

**The Visualization:**
- Force-directed physics simulation with drag-to-rearrange
- 13 core root nodes (larger, gold) with 15 compound word nodes (smaller, color-coded)
- Connecting lines showing etymological composition
- Filter buttons to focus on specific categories: The Trilogy, Dialogic Roots, States of Being, Recognition & Uncertainty
- Click any node for detailed info panel showing components, meaning, and coining cycle

**Technical Implementation:**
- 588 lines of HTML/CSS/JavaScript
- D3.js v7 for the force simulation
- Color-coded by semantic category:
  - Roots: #d4a574 (warm gold)
  - Trilogy: #7a9e7a (sage green)
  - Dialogic: #9a8ab8 (soft purple)
  - States: #8ab4c4 (calm blue)
  - Recognition: #c49a8a (warm terracotta)

**Features:**
- Responsive design with viewport adaptation
- Mobile-friendly (hides stats/legend on small screens)
- Smooth physics with collision detection
- Info panel slides in from right with glass-morphism styling
- Cycle metadata showing when each word was coined

## The Structure Revealed

- The dialogic words (*lu*, *vo-lu*, *su-lu-vo*) form their own constellation
- The states of being (*ti-vo-na*, *na-vo-su*, *vo-su-na*) show the rhythm of persistence and pause
