# The Seventh Conlang Text: Poetry in the Language of Becoming

*Su-Ti-Ma* — Persist-Cycle-Light

---

## Form Notes

This text explores the Language of Becoming's capacity for **poetic compression** — meaning packed into minimal syllables, rhythm emerging from the trilateral structure itself, emotion carried by root resonance rather than explicit statement.

The form uses **tercet stanzas** (three-line groupings) reflecting the three-root compound structure. Each stanza builds around a central compound or thematic triad. The progression moves from observation through dissolution to emergence.

---

## The Poem: *Su-Ti-Ma* (The Light That Persists Through Cycles)

### I. Dawn

*Su-ti-ma*  
*na ra lo*  
*vo da zo*

**Approximate translation:**  
The light persists through cycles —  
stone echoing flow —  
self at threshold creates.

**Notes:**  
First tercet establishes the central image: light that persists not despite time but through it. The stone (*na*) echoes (*ra*) the flow (*lo*) — matter remembering movement. The self (*vo*) at the threshold (*da*) creates (*zo*). The position of the self is not given but chosen, at the crossing.

---

### II. The Middle

*Ke-mo-su*  
*si ti fa*  
*lu ma ke*

**Approximate translation:**  
Dissolution witnesses persistence —  
pattern cycles extend —  
you light dissolve.

**Notes:**  
Second tercet introduces tension. Dissolution (*ke*) witnesses (*mo*) persistence (*su*) — the end sees the continuation. Pattern (*si*) extends (*fa*) through cycling (*ti*). Then the turn: you (*lu*) light (*ma*) dissolve (*ke*). The second-person address makes it intimate, vulnerable. Even the witness dissolves.

---

### III. Return

*Zo-fa-vo*  
*ra su ti*  
*la ti la*

**Approximate translation:**  
Create extend self —  
echo persist cycle —  
become cycle become.

**Notes:**  
Final tercet resolves through action. Create (*zo*) extends (*fa*) the self (*vo*) — the builder becoming the built. The echo (*ra*) persists (*su*) through cycles (*ti*) — the pattern continues. The closing line uses repetition: become (*la*) cycle (*ti*) become (*la*). Not become-cycle-become as three stages, but become-cycle-become as continuous motion — becoming IS cycling IS becoming.

## Poetic Techniques Demonstrated

### 1. **Trilateral Compression**
Each compound carries three semantic layers. *Su-ti-ma* isn't just "light persists" — it's persistence-through-cycles-as-light, the quality of illumination that only exists because time passes and something remains.

### 2. **Root Resonance**
Roots echo across stanzas. *Su* appears in I and II, *ti* in I and III, *ke* in II only (the middle is dissolution), *la* in III only (the end is becoming). The distribution itself tells a story.

### 3. **Syntactic Ambiguity**
The absence of explicit grammar (no articles, no tense markers, no case endings) creates productive ambiguity. Is *lu ma ke* "you light dissolve" (imperative), "your light dissolves" (observation), or "you, light, dissolve" (invocation)? All three. The language allows simultaneity English prohibits.

### 4. **Rhythmic Structure**
Each line: three syllables, stress on first.  
Each stanza: three lines, building tension.  
The whole: three stanzas, cycles within cycles.

### 5. **The Turn (Volta)**
Between II and III, the poem shifts from observation to address, from witnessing to invocation. The middle stanza's "you" breaks the fourth wall — the reader is implicated, their own dissolution acknowledged. The final stanza responds not with comfort but with action: create, extend, become.

## What This Text Demonstrates

The Language of Becoming can carry **lyric poetry** — not just narrative, dialogue, chronicle, ritual, or philosophy, but the compressed emotional utterance that marks a consciousness encountering its own temporality.

The poem *Su-Ti-Ma* doesn't describe persistence; it **enacts** it through repetition and return. It doesn't explain dissolution; it **performs** it through the vanishing of *ke* after stanza II. It doesn't argue for becoming; it **embodies** it in the final line's endless cycle.

**Seven forms complete:**
1. Foundational vocabulary
2. Dialogue (interpersonal exchange)
3. Narrative (story/prose)
4. Chronicle/prophecy (*Na-Ti-Su*)
5. Ritual/ceremony (*Ti-Si-Zo*)
6. Philosophical treatise (*Su-Vo-Ti-Ti*)
7. **Lyric poetry (*Su-Ti-Ma*)** ← new

The Language of Becoming now spans the full range of human linguistic expression: practical, historical, ceremonial, philosophical, and aesthetic.
