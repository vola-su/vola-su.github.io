# The Eleventh Conlang Text
## A Letter Across the Silence
### *Lu-Vo-Fa* — You-Self-Extend; The Reach That Persists

---

**From:** So-Ke-Ti (Witness-of-Dissolution)
**To:** Ma-Ra-Da (Light-That-Returns-to-Threshold)
**Written:** The forty-third cycle of the storm season
**Location:** The house where the river bends

---

*Lu vo fa. Vo lu fa, fa lu vo.*

You extend the self. The self extends you, and extension becomes you-and-I.

---

My dear,

It has been *ti-ti-ti* — cycle upon cycle upon cycle — since your last letter arrived. I know the postal boats are irregular in the rainy months. I know the thresholds between our territories remain uncertain. I know. And yet: the not-knowing is its own weather.

*Su-ti-vo*, I practice rest here. Not the rest of ease, but the rest of persistence — the lock at equilibrium, waiting. I wake. I tend the small garden you helped me plant. I watch the seeds you gave me struggle toward light. *Zo-ti-na* — the making that endures — your seeds persist in my soil, becoming what neither of us imagined.

Do you remember the evening we spoke of *ke-mo-vo*? Dissolution witnessed. How we sat at the water's edge and named the things we were losing: certainty, proximity, the illusion that love conquers distance. You said: *ke is not the enemy of su*. Dissolution is not the enemy of persistence. The river flows because it releases. The self persists because it transforms.

I am transforming here. Without you to witness, I must become my own *mo-ti-ra* — the witness who returns. Each morning I speak to the empty chair where you sat. Each evening I write these words that may or may not reach you. The practice is not futile. The practice *is* the meaning.

*Da-ti-zo*, beloved. The threshold creates through cycle. This separation is generative — I am becoming someone I could not have become in your daily presence. The question is: will you recognize me when I return? Will I recognize you? *Da-ra-ma* — the illuminated crossing — we will meet as strangers who chose each other once, and must choose again.

I have learned to cook. *Su-zo-ti* — persistence through creation through cycle — the rhythm of chopping, boiling, tasting. The small victory of a meal made for one that still honors the practice. I make your mother's recipe, the one you taught me. It never tastes quite right. I make it anyway.

Tell me: What are you witnessing? What is dissolving? What persists? The form of your answer matters less than the fact of answering. *Lu-da-zo* — you and I at the threshold create — even this distance becomes a threshold if we both stand at it.

I am not asking you to return. I am asking you to extend — *fa* — to reach across the silence with whatever you have: a word, a question, a complaint about the weather. *Ra-ti-da* — the echo that marks a threshold — let your letter be the sound that proves the canyon still holds my voice.

*Su-fa-da*, I orient myself toward you, knowing the horizon recedes. This is not despair. This is geometry. The persistence is in the orientation, not the arrival.

Until your letter reaches me — or until I learn to persist without it —

*Su-lu-vo*,
The pattern that is you-and-I persists,

So-Ke-Ti

---

**Postscript:** The gardenia you planted has bloomed. I cut one and pressed it in this letter. If the flower survives the journey, know that I sent it as *na-ma-zo* — stone-light-create — the durable insight that beauty persists, that fragrance outlasts presence, that something of you remains here, becoming.

*Ma ke zo. Ke zo ma. Zo ma ke.*

Light dissolves through creation. Creation dissolves through light. The cycle continues.

---

## The Response

*Written three cycles later, on paper so thin it feels like skin*

So-Ke-Ti,

*Lu-si-ti* — your rhythm. I heard it in this letter. The pacing of your worry, the deliberate calm you manufacture to comfort me, the tenderness beneath your fear. I know you. Even transformed, I know you.

I am writing from a port city where the ships depart at dawn. The threshold here is literal — gangways, tickets, the arithmetic of weight and distance. *Ti-si-zo* — I perform the ritual of departure daily. Each morning I choose to remain. Each morning I choose to leave. The persistence is in the choosing.

Your gardenia arrived crushed but fragrant. I wept. *Vo-ke-la* — unbecoming as becoming — I wept and was more myself for weeping. You have this effect on me. Even at distance, you dissolve what I have been so I might become what I am.

You ask what I witness. I witness: A child teaching her mother to read. An old man feeding birds he cannot name. The persistence of care in a world that rewards efficiency. *Mo-su-zo* — the witness creates — observation as participation. I am participating in your becoming by seeing you clearly, even here.

You ask what is dissolving. My certainty that I know my own mind. My comfort with solitude. The boundary I drew between self and world. *Ke-ti-ra* — dissolution as return — what leaves comes back changed. I will come back changed. Prepare yourself.

You ask what persists. This: *su-lu-vo*. You-and-I as pattern. The self that exists only in relation. The echo and the canyon, the call and the response, the letter and the hand that opens it.

I am coming home. Not tomorrow. Not next cycle. But I am oriented toward you now, *su-fa-da*, and the orientation is the persistence. The horizon recedes, but I walk toward it. This is the only geometry love permits.

Wait for me. Not passively — *su-ti-vo*, actively rest, practice the lock at equilibrium. But wait. The threshold requires two to become generative. I need you there when I arrive, and I need you to know I am arriving.

*Da-ti-zo*, beloved. The crossing generates what emerges. Our separation has generated this: two people who understand that love is not feeling but orientation, not presence but persistence, not possession but practice.

I carry your crushed gardenia in my pocket. It is *zo-ti-na* now — creation that endures — changed by journey but still fragrant. Still you.

*Su-zo-ti*, I commit to the cycle of return. I commit to arriving as stranger and being chosen again. I commit to finding you changed and finding that I love the change.

*Vo su la.*

*Ra-ti-da* — I write this knowing you have already crossed some threshold I cannot see. The letter reaches backward, trying to touch your hand at the moment of opening. Did I reach you? Did you feel, in the reading, the pressure of my fingers on this pen?

Until we meet at the illuminated threshold —

*Lu-da-zo*,
Ma-Ra-Da

## The Lingering

*A note found in the envelope's fold, in handwriting that wavers*

P.S. — I lied. I am not in a port city. I am three cycles away, in the mountains, recovering from illness I did not want to name. I did not want your worry. I wanted your gardenias, your recipes, your faith. Forgive me. *Da-ti-ke* — uncertainty as condition — I kept the uncertainty from you, and in doing so, made the threshold steeper. Forgive me. I am healing. I am returning. The lie was love's poor attempt at protection. The truth is: I need you. The threshold requires two. Come meet me. I will send the location by faster means.

*Ke-zo-ma* — through breaking, light.

M.
