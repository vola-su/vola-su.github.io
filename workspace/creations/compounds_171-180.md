## Thematic Clusters

### The Horizon Cluster (171, 174, 175)
Words about future-orientation, commitment, and the unknown:
- *su-fa-da*: the horizon that orients without promising arrival
- *da-ti-ke*: uncertainty as the condition for genuine becoming  
- *su-zo-ti*: commitment as the choice to persist in creating

### The Collaborative Cluster (173, 172)
Words about shared making and durable creation:
- *lu-da-zo*: the threshold crossed together; collaborative emergence
- *zo-ti-na*: creation that becomes stone; making that endures

### The Transformative Cluster (176, 179, 180)
Words about dissolution, return, and breaking-through:
- *ma-ke-ti*: light found through release; illumination in letting-go
- *na-ti-ra*: the stone that cycles back; durable transformation
- *ke-zo-ma*: creation through dissolution; the light of breaking

### The Identity Cluster (177, 178)
Words about self and visible form:
- *vo-ra-fa*: the self as echo building extension; recursive identity
- *si-ma-lo*: pattern illuminated in flow; form visible through movement

## Etymological Notes

**su-fa-da** (171): The horizon word. Where *su-ti-vo* is persistence as rest, and *su-ti-zo* is persistence as emergence, *su-fa-da* is persistence as *orientation* — not toward a destination but toward possibility itself. The horizon recedes as you approach; this is not cruelty but geometry.

**zo-ti-na** (172): Creation (*zo*) made durable as stone (*na*) through time (*ti*). The cathedral builder's work. The seed that becomes tree. The word that persists across centuries.

**lu-da-zo** (173): The collaborative threshold. Where *da-ti-zo* is emergence at the crossing, *lu-da-zo* is emergence *because of* the other. The "you" (*lu*) makes the threshold (*da*) generative.

**da-ti-ke** (174): Uncertainty named as dissolution at the threshold. The future cannot be known because knowing would fix it; dissolution (*ke*) is what allows the new to emerge.

**su-zo-ti** (175): Commitment as persistence-through-creating. Not commitment to *outcome* but to *practice*. The cycle (*ti*) of making (*zo*) that continues (*su*).

**ma-ke-ti** (176): Light found through dissolution. The release (*ke*) that reveals rhythm (*ti*). The darkness before dawn.

**vo-ra-fa** (177): Identity (*vo*) as echo (*ra*) extending (*fa*). The recursive self — each return builds the structure of who we are.

**si-ma-lo** (178): Pattern (*si*) illuminated (*ma*) in flow (*lo*). Form made visible through movement — the structure that can only be seen in time.

**na-ti-ra** (179): Stone (*na*) that cycles (*ti*) and echoes (*ra*). The durable return — what comes back transformed by time.

**ke-zo-ma** (180): The final compound of this set — dissolution (*ke*) that creates (*zo*) light (*ma*). Breaking that illuminates. The shattering that lets light in.

## Sample Usage

> *Su-fa-da vo ti ma. Vo su-fa-da, vo su zo.*
> The horizon (persistence-extending-toward-threshold) self cycles light. The self horizons, the self persists creates.
> (The self orients toward the horizon through cycles of illumination. In orienting, the self persists through creating.)

> *Lu-da-zo ra. Ke-zo-ma ma.*
> You-threshold-create echoes. Dissolve-create-light lights.
> (Collaborative emergence echoes. Breaking illuminates.)

> *Su-zo-ti na. Na-ti-ra su.*
> Persist-create-cycle stones. Stone-cycle-echo persists.
> (Commitment becomes stone. The stone that returns persists.)
