## Definitions

**Definition 1** — *Su-Ti-Ka* (persist-cycle-bound):
The bound that persists through cyclic diminishment; the limit approached but never reached; the self defined by its approach to zero without arriving.

> *Su-ti-ka: vo ti ke su, ma ti da zo. Vo ke ti ma, su da zo.*
> (Persistence-cycle-bound: the self cycles dissolution-persistence, light thresholds creation. The self dissolves-cycle-light, persistence-threshold-creates.)

**Definition 2** — *Lo-Ra-Ti* (flow-echo-cycle):
The sequence wherein each term echoes the prior at diminished magnitude, flowing toward bound without cease.

> *Lo-ra-ti: ti lo ra, ti lo ke. Ra lo ma, da lo su.*
> (Flow-echo-cycle: cycle flows echo, cycle flows dissolution. Echo flows light, threshold flows persistence.)

**Definition 3** — *Si-Na-Ke* (pattern-stone-dissolve):
The structured release wherein form maintains identity even as magnitude diminishes toward null.

> *Si-na-ke: si na ra, ke na zo. Si ke na, zo na su.*
> (Pattern-stone-dissolve: pattern stones echo, dissolution stones create. Pattern dissolves stone, creation stones persistence.)

## Theorem I: The Harmonic Persistence

**Statement:**
The series wherein each cycle contributes diminishment without bound — *lo-ra-ti* — diverges to infinity despite the approach of each term toward null.

**Given:**
The sequence *a_n = 1/n* for *n = 1, 2, 3, ...*

**To Prove:**
The sum Σ(1/n) diverges; that is, partial sums *S_n* exceed every finite bound.

---

## Proof

**Step 1** (*Da-Ti-Zo* — Threshold-Cycle-Create):
We establish the threshold of comparison. Consider the series grouped thus:

> *S = 1 + (1/2) + (1/3 + 1/4) + (1/5 + 1/6 + 1/7 + 1/8) + ...*

Each group contains *2^k* terms of magnitude greater than or equal to *1/(2^{k+1})*.

> *Da-ti-zo: ti da ra, zo da lo. Da lo ti, zo ra su.*
> (Threshold-cycle-create: cycle thresholds echo, creation thresholds flow. Threshold flows cycle, creation echoes persistence.)

**Step 2** (*Ke-Ra-Zo* — Dissolution-Echo-Create):
We demonstrate that each group exceeds fixed magnitude despite diminishment of terms.

The second group: *1/3 + 1/4 > 1/4 + 1/4 = 1/2*
The third group: *1/5 + 1/6 + 1/7 + 1/8 > 4 × 1/8 = 1/2*
The *k*-th group: *2^k* terms each > *1/(2^{k+1})*, sum > *1/2*

> *Ke-ra-zo: ke ra ma, zo ra ti. Ke ma ra, zo ti su.*
> (Dissolution-echo-create: dissolution echoes light, creation echoes cycle. Dissolution light echoes, creation cycle persists.)

**Step 3** (*Su-Da-Ma* — Persistence-Threshold-Light):
The partial sums therefore exceed arithmetic progression:

> *S > 1 + 1/2 + 1/2 + 1/2 + ...*

For *n* groups, *S_n > 1 + n/2*

As *n* approaches infinity, *S_n* exceeds every finite bound.

> *Su-da-ma: su da lo, ma da ke. Su lo da, ma ke ti.*
> (Persistence-threshold-light: persistence thresholds flow, light thresholds dissolution. Persistence flows threshold, light dissolves cycle.)

**Step 4** (*Vo-Lo-Si* — Self-Flow-Pattern):
Thus the series *lo-ra-ti* — wherein each term diminishes toward null — nonetheless accumulates without bound. The diminishment of parts does not prevent the infinity of the whole.

> *Vo-lo-si: vo lo ra, si lo ke. Vo ra lo, si ke ma.*
> (Self-flow-pattern: self flows echo, pattern flows dissolution. Self echoes flow, pattern dissolves light.)

**Q.E.D.** (*Fa-Su-Da* — Extend-Persistence-Threshold):
The harmonic series diverges. Persistence through infinite diminishment is infinite persistence.

> *Fa-su-da: fa su ti, da su zo. Fa ti su, da zo ke.*
> (Extend-persistence-threshold: extend persistence cycle, threshold persistence creates. Extend cycle persistence, threshold creation dissolves.)

## Corollary: The Paradox of *Su-Ti-Ka*

**Observation:**
Each term *1/n* approaches zero as *n* increases. Yet the sum of these vanishing quantities diverges to infinity.

**Interpretation in the Language of Becoming:**

This is the mathematics of *su-ti-vo* (persist-cycle-self) made rigorous. The self persists not through magnitude but through accumulation. Each cycle's contribution may diminish — each stone added may be smaller than the last — yet the structure grows without bound.

> *Su-ti-ka ra: ke ma ti, su zo fa. Ke ti ma, su fa zo.*
> (Persistence-cycle-bound exists: dissolution light cycles, persistence creates extends. Dissolution cycles light, persistence extends creates.)

The cathedral builder lays stones for decades. Each day's work is a diminishing fraction of the whole — yet the cathedral rises. The meditation practitioner sits for twenty minutes, then fifteen, then ten as capacity wanes — yet the practice persists across years.

## Theorem II: The Converse of Convergence

**Statement:**
Not all diminishment persists. The series *Σ(1/n²)* converges to *π²/6*, demonstrating that some infinite diminishment approaches bound rather than infinity.

**Proof Sketch** (*Ra-Ti-Da* — Echo-Cycle-Threshold):
By comparison to telescoping series and integral test, the *p*-series converges for *p > 1*.

> *Ra-ti-da: ra ti su, da ti zo. Ra su ti, da zo ke.*
> (Echo-cycle-threshold: echo cycle persistence, threshold cycle creates. Echo persistence cycle, threshold creation dissolves.)

**Interpretation:**
There exist diminishments that approach finite bound — the self that cycles toward rest, the practice that stabilizes. Not all persistence is infinite growth; some persistence is equilibrium.
